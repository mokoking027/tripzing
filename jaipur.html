The Pink CityJaipur – Royal Heritage

Explore the Kingdom
Jaipur, known as the "Pink City," is the capital of Rajasthan, India, founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. Famous for its iconic terracotta-colored buildings, it is India’s first planned city, blending Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural styles. As a major tourist destination, it offers stunning landmarks like the City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amber Fort, while serving as a vibrant hub for culture, jewelry, and textiles.

3-Day Royal Journey

Day 1: Arrival & Architecture

Forts & Palaces
  • Check in to hotel 'Holiday Inn', get ready and go for breakfast at 10 AM.
  • A black XUV will pick you up from the location at 11 AM.
  • Your first spot will be the Amer Fort. Experience the majestic views from the top and click beautiful pictures. 🏰
  • Head to the iconic Hawa Mahal and visit the beautiful City Palace nearby. 🌬️
  • Around 2 PM, have lunch at Wind View Cafe, offering a surreal view of the mahal.
  • Head back to your hotel to relax and refresh.
  • At 4 PM, leave for the astronomical observatory, Jantar Mantar. 🔭
  • In the evening around 6 PM, explore the famous Johari Bazaar and try local street food. 🛍️

Day 2: Vistas & Culture

Hills & Heritage
  • Have a hearty breakfast in the hotel at 9:30 AM.
  • Visit the Nahargarh Fort, including the famous Sheesh Mahal and Wax Museum. 🌄
  • Explore the nearby Jaigarh Fort, a prominent historical destination. 🏯
  • Around 2 PM, have a luxurious lunch at Suvarna Mahal restaurant. Afterwards, take a local stroll in the city on e-rickshaws.
  • In the evening, visit the beautiful Jal Mahal (Water Palace), stunningly illuminated by evening lights. 🌊
  • The final stop is a cultural evening at Chokhi Dhani. Experience authentic Rajasthani themes and relish the famous Rajasthani Thali. 🎭

Day 3: Museums & Markets

Shopping & Departure
  • In the morning, visit the stunning Albert Hall Museum. 🏛️
  • Visit the serene Birla Mandir and grab breakfast at local stalls featuring famous Kachori and Lassi. 🕊️
  • Proceed to Bapu Bazaar for traditional shopping (auto-rickshaw recommended due to crowds). 🛒
  • Return to the hotel for a relaxing dinner in the evening. 🍛
  • The next morning, the car will drop you off at the airport.

Cuisine of Jaipur

Dal Baati Churma

A timeless Rajasthani classic with baked baati, spiced dal, and sweet churma served together.

Ghewar

A festive honeycomb dessert topped with saffron, rabri, and nuts for rich sweetness.

Laal Maas

Fiery mutton curry cooked with red chilies and robust spices, iconic to Rajasthan.

Pyaaz Kachori

Crispy flaky pastry stuffed with spicy onion masala, a beloved Jaipur street snack.

Ker Sangri

A desert-specialty sabzi of berries and beans, tangy, earthy, and deeply traditional.

Mawa Kachori

Sweet fried kachori filled with mawa and dry fruits, finished with fragrant sugar syrup.

Essential Travel Tips

The best time to visit Jaipur is from October to March when the weather is pleasant. Always bargain at local markets like Johari and Bapu Bazaar. Wear comfortable walking shoes for the forts, and stay hydrated. Auto-rickshaws and e-rickshaws are the best way to navigate crowded streets.
