Leh-Ladakh Itinerary

Journey through the Himalayas — a soulful exploration of high-altitude deserts, cobalt lakes, and ancient monasteries.

The Mountain Journal

7 DAYS • 11,500 FT ALTITUDE • INFINITE MEMORIES

01

Arrival

Acclimatization in Leh

  • Welcome at Leh Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport.
  • Check-in at 'Grand Himalaya Luxury Hotel' and mandatory rest for acclimatization.
  • Light evening walk in Leh Market for local crafts and traditional dinner.

02

Local

Monasteries & Palaces

  • Visit the iconic Shanti Stupa for panoramic valley views.
  • Explore the historical Leh Palace and its museum of royal artifacts.
  • Afternoon lunch at 'Tibetan Kitchen' for authentic Himalayan flavors.
  • Evening visit to the Hall of Fame to honor the Himalayan defenders.

03

Nubra

Khardung La to Nubra Valley

  • The drive of a lifetime via Khardung La Pass (one of the world's highest motorable roads).
  • Breathtaking stopovers for tea and mountain vistas.
  • Arrival at Nubra Valley and check-in at the serene 'Hotel Grand Nubra'.

04

Dunes

Sand Dunes & Diskit

  • Visit the majestic Diskit Monastery and its giant Maitreya Buddha.
  • Camel safari on the double-humped Bactrian camels at Hunder Sand Dunes.
  • Evening cultural program and local organic dinner at the camp.

05

Pangong

The Azure Pangong Lake

  • Traverse the rugged terrain to reach the legendary Pangong Tso.
  • Experience the lake's famous color-changing waters (from blue to green).
  • Overnight stay in luxury tents under the clearest starlit sky.

06

Return

Pangong to Leh Return

  • Morning stroll by the lake for sunrise photography.
  • Visit the local nomadic markets for pashmina and woollens.
  • Drive back to Leh via Chang La Pass, returning to the hotel by evening.

07

Farewell

The Final Mountain Breath

  • Final Himalayan breakfast with a view of the peaks.
  • Check-out and transfer to the airport with a heart full of Leh.

Mountain Wisdom

ESSENTIAL TIPS FOR THE TRAIL

🏔️

Acclimatization

Resting on the first day is not an option, it's a necessity. Avoid heavy activities to let your body adjust to the thin air.

💧

Hydration

Drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily. Hydration is the best defense against Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS).

🧥

Layering

The mountain sun is harsh, but the wind is biting. Always wear layers even if it looks sunny outside.
